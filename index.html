THE OVERDOSE CRISIS IN AMERICA

fast facts

Overdose is the leading cause of death for Americans under 55, above car crashes and gun violence. Overdoses have increased in all drug categories, male and female, all ethnicities, all areas of urbanization, aged 15+. In 2017, 72,000 people died from a drug overdose.

4/5 heroin users reported beginning to use heroin after becoming addicted to opioid painkillers. From 1999 to 2011 hydrocodone consumption more than doubled, and oxycodone consumption increased by nearly 500%. During that time frame, opioid-related OD death rate nearly quadrupled.
